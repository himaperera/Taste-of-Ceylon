Watalappan

Traditional Sri Lankan coconut custard pudding with jaggery and spices

View Recipe

Cultural Significance

Watalappan is a beloved Sri Lankan dessert with strong Malay influences, traditionally prepared by the Sri Lankan Moor and Malay communities. This rich, creamy custard is especially popular during Ramadan and other festive occasions, symbolizing celebration and togetherness.

The name "Watalappan" comes from the Tamil word "vattil" meaning "cup" and "appam" meaning "cake" - referring to the traditional method of steaming in small cups or bowls. The unique combination of coconut milk, jaggery, and aromatic spices creates a dessert that's uniquely Sri Lankan.

45 mins Prep & Cook Time
6-8 servings Servings
Steamed Cooking Method
4.8 Rating

Ingredients

  • 6 large eggs
  • 200g jaggery (kitul or coconut), grated or chopped
  • 400ml thick coconut milk
  • 100ml thin coconut milk
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1/4 tsp nutmeg, freshly grated
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp vanilla essence (optional)
  • 2 tbsp cashew nuts, chopped (for garnish)
  • 2 tbsp raisins (for garnish)
  • 1 tbsp ghee or butter (for greasing)

Instructions

  1. Prepare Jaggery Syrup: In a small saucepan, melt the jaggery with thin coconut milk over low heat. Stir continuously until completely dissolved. Strain to remove any impurities and let it cool to room temperature.
  2. Preheat Steamer: Fill a steamer or large pot with water and bring to a boil. Grease 6-8 small heatproof bowls or ramekins with ghee or butter.
  3. Mix Eggs: In a large mixing bowl, beat the eggs lightly until well combined but not frothy.
  4. Combine Ingredients: Slowly add the cooled jaggery syrup to the beaten eggs, whisking continuously. Add thick coconut milk, cardamom powder, nutmeg, cinnamon, salt, and vanilla essence (if using). Mix well until everything is thoroughly combined.
  5. Strain Mixture: Strain the mixture through a fine sieve to ensure a smooth texture and remove any egg strands or spice particles.
  6. Fill Containers: Pour the strained mixture into the greased bowls, filling them about 3/4 full to allow space for rising.
  7. Add Garnish: Sprinkle chopped cashew nuts and raisins on top of each portion.
  8. Steam: Carefully place the bowls in the steamer. Cover with a lid and steam for 25-30 minutes over medium heat. Alternatively, you can use a pressure cooker (without the weight) for 15-20 minutes.
  9. Check Doneness: Insert a toothpick or skewer into the center of the watalappan. If it comes out clean, it's done. The custard should be set but still slightly wobbly in the center.
  10. Cool and Serve: Remove from steamer and let cool to room temperature. Serve warm or chilled. Watalappan can be refrigerated for up to 3 days.

Chef's Tips

Jaggery Selection

Use good quality kitul or coconut jaggery for authentic flavor. Palm jaggery can also be used but will give a different taste.

Steaming Technique

Ensure the steamer is properly heated before adding the bowls. Maintain consistent medium heat throughout cooking.

Smooth Texture

Straining the mixture is crucial for achieving the characteristic smooth, silky texture of traditional watalappan.

Serving Suggestions

Serve chilled for a refreshing dessert or warm for a comforting treat. Garnish with extra nuts and a sprinkle of cinnamon.
